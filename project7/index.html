Cantopop: The Golden Year Of Generations

Cantopop (a contraction of "Cantonese pop music") or HK-pop (short for "Hong Kong pop music") is a genre of pop music written in standard Chinese and sung in Cantonese.

The concept and term "Cantopop" was born in the 1970s to describe Cantonese pop music in Hong Kong. At that time, Cantonese was still considered a language below English and Mandarin by the colonial society, and Cantonese songs were still on the periphery. Throughout the 1970s, with the rise of Hong Kong's television and film industries, Hong Kong music also became popular and began to symbolize the city's extraordinarily diverse culture. The eighties were the golden age of Hong Kong music, and this glory continued into the early nineties until it began to fade in the face of the gradual prevalence of Mandarin pop and the recent explosion of K-pop. However, there are still a large number of post-80s, post-90s, and even post-00s young people who are very fond of Hong Kong music. Although the glory is fading away, the sentiment of the golden years contained in Hong Kong music is still moving.
